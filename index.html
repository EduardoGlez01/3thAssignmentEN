How Trump's speech could have driven the formation of hate speech groups

Specialists agree that the proliferation of hate speech messages on social media by right-wing politicians such as the former president could have encouraged the articulation of these groups.

By Eduardo González | Dec. 20, 2022
Leelo en Español

Between the time former President Donald Trump announced his candidacy in June 2015 and halfway through his administration at the end of 2018, the presence of hate groups in the United States grew 12.55 percent, according to records from the Southern Poverty Law Center.

2015

States like as Texas and California clustered already the largest amount of these organizations

2018

Hate groups were articulated where previously there were, in states such as Alaska and Hawaii.

2021

At the end of the administration, the presence of these groups dropped 39 percent nationwide.

For some academic researchers, the proliferation of these messages of ultra-American sentiment are key to the success of right-wing figures like the former president.

"Trump built up a high level of aggression among his followers step-by-step using social media, speeches, interviews, press conferences, etc. He is a highly mediated far-right politicians who cannot survive politically without having networked communication technologies at his disposal".

Christian Fuchs, University of Westminster
